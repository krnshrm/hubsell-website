coreDifference: "<p>The difference comes down to two things: where the data comes from, and whether your channels run together.</p><p>Apollo is built around a pre-built database. Apollo describes it as roughly 275 million contacts collected from a network of two million contributors, public web crawling, and third-party providers, with monthly checks to keep it current. You pay per seat, then spend credits each time you unlock, export, or enrich a record. The data was gathered before you went looking for it.</p><p>hubsell works the other way around. There's no stored database to pull from. When you run a search, hubsell sources the contacts for that search and validates them at that moment. The record is built for your query, not pulled from a list someone else compiled months earlier.</p><p>The second difference is coordination. In Apollo, email steps run automatically, but LinkedIn steps are task reminders. Apollo's own documentation states that LinkedIn tasks have to be completed manually and that Apollo does not complete them for you. In January 2026, Apollo removed automated LinkedIn actions from the platform entirely. So a rep runs email on autopilot and works LinkedIn by hand.</p><p>In hubsell, email and LinkedIn run as one sequence. Connection requests, profile visits, DMs, InMail, and follow-ups execute in the same flow as your emails, with conditional steps based on what each contact does. One flow, one set of rules, one place to see what happened.</p>"

dataQuality: "<p>Every contact database decays. People change jobs every two to three years, companies merge, domains change. Industry estimates put B2B data decay at roughly 25 to 30 percent a year. A database checked monthly is, for most of any given month, working from records that have already started to age.</p><p>Apollo advertises a 97 percent email accuracy rate. It's worth knowing how that's measured. Inside Apollo, you can apply a verified emails filter and watch the available pool shrink, which shows how much of the database carries a verified email at any moment. The verified label is something you can test yourself in the product before you rely on it.</p><p>hubsell removes the decay problem by not storing data in the first place. Contacts are sourced and validated at the moment you search, so the record is current when you use it, not current as of the last refresh cycle. Accuracy here means the address is live and deliverable at send time, which is the number that protects your sender reputation.</p>"
pricing: "<p>Apollo charges per seat, then per credit. Paid plans run from $49 to $119 per user per month on annual billing, and more month to month. Credits are consumed every time you unlock, export, or enrich a contact. They don't roll over, overages are billed automatically, and you can't reduce seats mid-contract.</p><p>hubsell is €120 per user per month, plus data as you use it: €0.10 per LinkedIn contact, and €0.50 per fully enriched and validated contact with a verified email. You pay for the contacts you source, not for a credit allowance you have to spend before it expires.</p><p>The structural difference: Apollo's data cost is a credit budget attached to a static database. hubsell's data cost is per contact, sourced live for your search.</p>"
chooseCompetitor: "<p>Apollo is the stronger choice if you want a large database you can browse and filter across dozens of attributes without sourcing each search, if cold calling is central to your motion and you want a built-in dialer, or if you want a free plan to start. For high-volume, email-first prospecting where you're set up to manage data freshness and deliverability yourself, Apollo's breadth is real and its filters are some of the best in the category.</p>"
chooseHubsell: "<p>hubsell is the stronger choice if your ICP sits in narrow verticals where big databases tend to run thin, if you want data that's fresh at the moment you use it rather than checked on a cycle, or if email and LinkedIn coordination matters and you don't want reps working LinkedIn by hand. It also fits teams that want one bill for the whole motion instead of a stack of tools, with every touch synced to the CRM automatically.</p>"
