05 — Blog (Insights)

A reading face for long-form

Recommendation: give the blog its own body face. Keep Lexend for post headings (brand stays intact), but set article body in Newsreader, a warm editorial serif. At length, a serif reads with less fatigue and signals "considered publication" rather than "marketing page," without leaving the palette. Marketing pages stay all-sans.

Insights

Why "verified at source" beats a bigger database

8 min read · B2B data

Most outbound starts cold. But the contact you bought last quarter has already changed jobs, changed numbers, or opted out — and you won't find out until the bounce. The instinct is to buy a bigger list. The fix is the opposite: source fewer contacts, later, and verify each one at the moment you actually need it.

You pay for a contact that's correct today, not for access to a list that was right two years ago.

A database decays from the day it's compiled. Live sourcing treats every record as a fresh question — asked the day you ask it, matched to your ICP, and validated against catch-all domains before a single credit is spent on outreach.
